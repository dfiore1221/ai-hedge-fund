# 2. Why Microsoft Might Be Attractive

## A. Strong revenue growth at massive scale

### Facts

Microsoft revenue increased from **$245.1B in FY2024** to **$281.7B in FY2025**.

That is approximately **14.9% year-over-year growth** based on the annual financial statements.

The summary dataset also lists **revenue growth of 18.3%**, but it does not specify whether that is trailing twelve months, year-over-year, or another calculation.

### Interpretation

For a company with a market cap near **$2.9 trillion**, mid-teens revenue growth is impressive. Large companies usually struggle to grow quickly because their revenue base is already enormous.

This makes Microsoft worth deeper research because the company may still be compounding at an attractive rate despite its size.

---

## B. Very high profitability

### Facts

In FY2025:

- Revenue: **$281.7B**
- Gross profit: **$193.9B**
- Operating income: **$128.5B**
- Net income: **$101.8B**

Approximate FY2025 margins:

| Margin Type | Approximate FY2025 Result |
|---|---:|
| Gross margin | **68.8%** |
| Operating margin | **45.6%** |
| Net margin | **36.1%** |

The summary dataset also reports a profit margin of **39.3%**, likely based on a different period or calculation.

### Interpretation

Microsoft has exceptional business economics. A company with nearly **46% operating margins** has significant pricing power, scale advantages, or a strong product mix.

For beginners: operating margin shows how much profit the company keeps from revenue before interest and taxes. A higher number usually means the company has a powerful business model.

---

## C. Strong return on equity

### Facts

The provided dataset lists Microsoft’s return on equity at **34.0%**.

### Interpretation

Return on equity measures how efficiently a company generates profit from shareholder capital. A 34% ROE is strong and suggests Microsoft converts its equity base into earnings efficiently.

This supports the view that Microsoft is not merely large, but also highly profitable relative to its capital base.

## D. Large operating cash flow

### Facts

Microsoft generated **$136.2B of operating cash flow** in FY2025.

Free cash flow from the cash flow statement was **$71.6B** in FY2025.

However, the summary field lists free cash flow as **$37.0B**, which conflicts with the annual cash flow statement.

### Interpretation

Microsoft generates enormous cash from operations. That gives the company flexibility to invest in infrastructure, fund research and development, return capital to shareholders, and maintain balance sheet strength.

But the free cash flow discrepancy is important. Before making an investment decision, we need to verify which free cash flow figure is current and why the summary field differs from the FY2025 cash flow statement.

# 3. Main Risks

## Risk 1: Capex intensity may pressure free cash flow

### Facts

Microsoft’s capital expenditure increased from **$44.5B in FY2024** to **$64.6B in FY2025**, an increase of about **45%**.

Operating cash flow increased from **$118.5B** to **$136.2B**, but free cash flow declined from **$74.1B** to **$71.6B**.

### Interpretation

This is one of the most important risks.

Microsoft is generating more operating cash, but capital spending is rising even faster. If AI/data-center investment does not generate strong returns, free cash flow growth could disappoint.

For a stock trading at a multi-trillion-dollar valuation, free cash flow disappointment can matter a lot.

---

## Risk 2: Valuation risk

### Facts

Microsoft trades at:

- Trailing P/E: **23.3x**
- Forward P/E: **20.2x**
- Market cap: **~$2.90T**

Using FY2025 free cash flow of **$71.6B**, the approximate free cash flow yield is about **2.5%**.

If the lower summary FCF figure of **$37.0B** were the relevant current number, the FCF yield would be much lower.

### Interpretation

Microsoft is not obviously cheap on free cash flow. Investors are likely paying for quality, durability, AI/cloud growth, and high margins.

That can be reasonable if growth continues, but it leaves less margin of safety if expectations fall.
